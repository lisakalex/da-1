What is a Crypto Presale?

Crypto presales, often referred to as ICOs (initial coin offerings), are usually the first time retail investors can buy tokens, also allowing them to secure digital assets before the token is listed on crypto exchanges.

A limited number of investors can purchase the token before it is offered to the wider public and during a presale, crypto tokens are likely to be sold at a highly discounted price – often via the platform’s website.

This is done not only to raise funds for development but to increase hype and interest around a newly launched project. Once the token is listed on an exchange, its price might go up. The investor can choose to sell the coin immediately for a profit, or they can continue holding the token and hope that the price continues to climb higher.

Ultimately, the strategy will depend on the project and the risk appetite of the investor. In addition to this, some projects reward early investors with premium memberships, additional tokens, and access to exclusive features.

Crypto presales can be private or public event, depending on the project. Some upcoming platforms require interested investors to register in advance to gain access to a crypto presale. On the other hand, there are also crypto presales that are accessible to the general public.

FAQs

What is a crypto presale?

Crypto presales are a popular way for investors to buy digital tokens at a discounted price. New cryptocurrencies might be offered to investors in a presale before they are launched via an ICO or listed on an exchange. New presale tokens may be announced on social media, blockchain news platforms, or crypto-related market aggregators.

How do I get presale crypto?

In most cases, the presale crypto can be purchased directly via the project’s official website. This will involve connecting a digital wallet to the website – beware of fake sites impersonating a project – and making a crypto transfer. Some platforms also allow investors to purchase presale crypto using credit/debit cards.

Are crypto presales ICOs?

No, a crypto presale is conducted before an ICO. However, the two terms are often used interchangeably, not least because they offer early access to a new crypto project. The ICO bubble of 2017, during the bull run of that year, involved a similar investment process to presales of today.

How can I tell if a presale is legitimate?

Most of the time, all of the information is clearly available in the whitepaper, website, and socials. Often, a project might state that it is for entertainment purposes only. If you read all the available information, you can make informed decisions, without falling prey to emotional investment.

What are the risks of presales?

One of the risks involved for investors in crypto presales is that you are investing in something new and unproven.
